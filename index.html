Part 1 & Part 2

In Part 1, the hovering/clicking function is employed. When hovering over the bar chart and the line graph, a popup displaying data values is shown.

In Part 2, a dropdown control panel option is utilized to modify the configuration of the chart. This dropdown enables users to switch the scatterplot display from depicting the correlation between atmospheric pressure and dewpoint temperature to showcasing atmospheric pressure and visibility.

Monthly Precipitation

This barchart illustrates the aggregated monthly precipitation in Atlanta from 2020 to 2022.

Pressure vs. Dewpoint

This scatterplot investigates the correlation between atmospheric pressure and dewpoint temperature (atmospheric pressure and visibility) in Atlanta during the 2020-2022 period. Additionally, it visualizes the temperature range difference in color and the precipitation amount in size.


Max and Min Temperature Over Time

This line graph visualizes the maximum and minimum temperatures in Atlanta over the three-year period (2020-2022), illustrating temperature trends.

Part 3

In Part 3, the scatterplots representing the fluctuations of dewpoint and temperature over time are linked together through brushing and linking functionality.
